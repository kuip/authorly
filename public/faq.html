Authorly - Frequently Asked Questions

What is Authorly?

Authorly is a cryptographic authorship verification tool that proves you wrote your essays, papers, and documents. It captures your complete writing process (every keystroke, edit, and revision) and creates tamper-proof evidence of your authorship.

Why do I need Authorly?

AI detectors are falsely accusing students of using AI to write their essays, even when they wrote them entirely by hand. These false positives can result in failed assignments, rejected college applications, and academic integrity violations. Authorly provides cryptographic proof that you wrote your work.

How does Authorly work?

Authorly records your entire writing process in real-time: every keystroke, deletion, edit, and revision. It creates a cryptographically-secured trail that cannot be tampered with, captures your unique typing patterns, and allows anyone to replay your writing process from blank page to finished document.

Why do AI detectors fail?

AI detectors are fundamentally flawed because AI models are trained on literary works and high-quality writing. When a student writes well using proper grammar and structure, it matches the patterns that AI learned from literature. This means good writing looks like AI writing, leading to false accusations. The better you write, the more likely you are to be flagged.

Can Authorly be used for collaborative writing?

Yes! Authorly tracks collaborative authorship and shows exact percentages of effort by each contributor. You can share your work with others and continue the authorship trail, with clear attribution for every contribution.

Is my writing private?

How is Authorly different from AI detectors?

AI detectors try to identify AI-generated text using probabilistic pattern matching, which is inherently unreliable. Authorly doesn't try to detect AI.
